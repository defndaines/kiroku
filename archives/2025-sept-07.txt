7 September Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

“Rapport” is the latest novella in the Murderbot series, but it focuses on characters from the second book. Murderbot is physically absent from this story, though referenced. Perihelion and its crew are on a new mission, and Peri starts using some of the tricks it learned from a certain rogue SecUnit. This is a little unsettling for the crew, especially as their mission gets more dangerous. My hold for book five should be ready in the next week or so.

I read “Katabasis” because it’s a book club selection this month. I’ve liked the two R.F. Kuang books I’ve read so far, but am lukewarm on her, since she writes unlikable characters. This book is no exception on that point. Alice Law and fellow grad student Peter travel to Hell to find their recently deceased adviser. There are extensive critiques of academia here, not just of Cambridge and Oxford. The imagining of Hell is intriguing, though the middle part of the book slogged for me. I liked to book, but am not inspired to read more of Kuang’s writing.

“Compulsory” is a short story which takes place before book one of the Murderbot series. It’s a quick vignette while Murderbot is working in a mine and only on episode 44 of “Sanctuary Moon”. The world is growing on me, after being initially just OK with the first book in the series. At this point, I’m planning to catch up with the whole series. After that, I want to check out some of Martha Wells’ other series, which I’ve heard good things about.
