## Fiction

“Sisters in Yellow” follows the life of Hana who, at 15, meets Kimiko. Kimiko is twenty years older, and she becomes something of a surrogate mother. They come together to run a bar that lives on the edge of legality. This is a slow novel, enmeshed in the lives of poor, working-class women in the late ’90s of Tōkyō. Our characters are not heroes, and not always likable in their behaviors. There’s a verisimilitude here that lays out the lives of women born with little and destined to stay that way. Recommended, though the book is not for everyone.

## Graphic Novels

“The Savage Sword of Conan #13” includes two solid stories. The Roy Thomas one feels classic, starting with a tale of thievery, but shifting on to a supernatural encounter that challenges Conan’s ability to survive. The Enrique Dueñas tale is short, a Solomon Kane story light on words but crafted quite well. Both are worthy renditions for Howard fans.
