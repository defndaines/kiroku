4 January Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

“A Magical Girl Retires” was my last read of 2025 and my second book by Park Seolyeon. A twenty-nine year old, who lost her job during the COVID-19 pandemic and is quickly approaching her credit limit, is interrupted in her suicide attempt by a representative of the magical-girl union. Is she the Magical Girl of Time, fated to save the world from its climate catastrophe? This novella doesn’t require that you know the magical-girl tropes it is playing with. It is at times funny and touching, with several poignant social critiques woven throughout. I didn’t like this as much as the first book I read by Park, but would still recommend it.

“Into the Drowning Deep” was my first read of 2026. Seven years ago, a film crew was lost over the Mariana Trench. Their death by “mermaids” was passed off as a hoax. In 2022, a new trip is assembled, including leading oceanographers, the younger sister of a victim of the first voyage, and a mermaid expert who knew this all would happen. Are they prepared for another attack? A fun and exciting read, a must-read for anyone who grew up with “Little Mermaid” tales. The cast of characters is a bit large, but everyone is distinct such that I didn’t have trouble tracking who was who, and not all of them are likable. The twists and interpretations on the legends are well done. Recommended for horror fans or anyone planning to snorkel or dive in the near future.
