Early-August Reading Wrap-up

Finally got to “The Dispossessed” by the great Ursula K. Le Guin. 5-star read that deservedly won the triple crown of sci-fi awards (Hugo, Nebula, Locus). Must-read exploration of how societies can be organized.

“My Brilliant Friend”, Elena Ferrante, was selected as the best novel so far of the 21st century by the New York Times, but it just didn’t do it for me. It was interesting to read about 1950s working-class Naples, but I honestly fell asleep multiple times while reading this. Won’t be completing the series.

“The Reckoning of Roku”, Randy Ribay, is the latest Avatar book. Fun read, though I preferred the two Kyoshi and first Yangchen books more. Worth reading for hardcore Avatar fans.

Of the several books I bought in Newfoundland, I started with “Send More Tourists...the Last Ones Were Delicious”, Tracey Waddleton. Great short story collection, more impactful for me with St. John’s fresh in my head.

The only graphic novel so far this month was “Olive, By the Light of a Blue Moon”, a French translation. The story had potential, but I just didn’t find the character or scenario engaging enough. I won’t be reading the rest of the series.
