29 December Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

“Bolla” is written by a Kosovo-born Finnish author, but the majority of the story takes place in Kosovo. Just before the outbreak of war in the 1990s, two young men start an affair. One is Serbian, while the other is Albanian and married. Perspective shifts between the characters as war comes and ends. The primary character, Arsim, is not sympathetic, but you see the toll homophobia takes on their lives.

“All My Rage” is told from three perspectives: two teenagers of Pakistani descent, and the mother of the male narrator retold from her youth and moving up to the current time. Set between Pakistan and Juniper, California, the main characters are high school seniors. The story is quite brutal to read at times, a real tearjerker as you get to know the broken people. The book won both the National Book Award and the Golden Poppy, both much deserved. This is contemporary fiction, but I’ll definitely check out Tahir’s fantasy work when I get a chance.

-- Nonfiction --


-- Graphic Novels and Manga --

“Chainsaw Man, Vol. 16: Ordinary Happiness” catches me up with all the volumes published now. The story got a bit more interesting in this one, with some of the hidden players finally coming to the surface a bit more.

“Freddie The Fix” is a fun story about a guy who fixes problems that supernatural folks get themselves into in and around L.A.

“The City Beneath Her Feet” is starting out with amazing art and a twist at the end that definitely sets up for a tale I want to read the next volume of.

“The Savage Sword of Conan” #6 is one of the better issues so far. The anchor story continues from the previous issue and pits Conan against sorcery in a way I hadn’t seen before. The other two tales, one prose the other graphic are better than average, too.
