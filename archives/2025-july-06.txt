6 July Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

“The Unwanteds” is my son’s current favorite series. I think he’s on book five of six now. I wanted to give it a try. It’s a dystopian future world where once a year, all thirteen-year-old kids of Quill are divided into three groups. The Wanteds become the elite, while the Unwanteds who have shown an ounce of creativity are culled. Aaron is selected as a Wanted, but his twin bother Alex is Unwanted. But when Alex arrives at the “death farm”, it turns out to be a hidden world run by Mr. Today, where the children learn magic. I can see the appeal to middle-grade readers, but the book doesn’t have a strong appeal for adults.

-- Nonfiction --

-- Graphic Novels and Manga --

“My Favorite Thing Is Monsters, Book Two” is the second half of the story of 10-year-old Karen Reyes, set in the summer of 1968. She continues her investigation of the events of the first book and makes some new friends. It’s the same amazing spiral-bound notebook style and a touchingly real story. It’s hard to say much more without giving away Book One. Highly recommended. It’s my fifth graphic story of the Hugo finalists, and I think it will be my top choice.

“Monstress, Volume 2: The Blood” continues the “Monstress” story, as Maika retraces some of her mother’s steps to understand why she is the way she is. This won the 2018 Hugo for Graphic Story, which is why I’m continuing with a series I’m not fully committed to. I remain ambivalent after this issue, too. I’ve started Volume 3, but will probably jump to the latest volume if that one doesn’t pull me in a bit more.
