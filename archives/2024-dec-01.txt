1 December Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

“Wandering Stars”, Tommy Orange (Cheyenne and Arapaho), is a follow-up to “There There”, which I read in 2020. Though, you don’t really have to have read or remember much of the earlier novel to appreciate this one. Here we delve deeply into generational trauma as well as addiction.

The best read this week was “Afterparties”, Anthony Veasna So, a collection of Cambodian-American short stories. Most of the stories focus on characters in their twenties, the children of those who fled the genocide. Characters deal with generational trauma and identity, with genuinely funny moments to boot.

-- Nonfiction --

“My Body Is a Book of Rules”, Elissa Washuta (Cowlitz), is a memoir about bipolar disorder and rape. It is NOT an easy read, but recommended if you can handle the difficult subject matter. It also has an academic flare, using footnotes to augment earlier writing and engage the reader with the text in a different way. And that cover picture!

“California Against the Sea: Visions for Our Vanishing Coastline”, Rosanna Xia, looks at various threats to the California coast. I think I’d read articles a couple of the chapters are derived from. We see the conflict of interest that private landholders present against having a sane relationship with a coast that is constantly changing. Very real examples of how all California residents are forced to subsidize the rich’s extravagant expectations that we prop up their property values. Recommended for California residents.

-- Graphic Novels and Manga --

“Chainsaw Man, Vol. 5: Minor”, Tatsuki Fujimoto, is, well, Chainsaw Man. This is the first volume that goes beyond the anime. Looking it up, it looks like part of this book is what will be in the movie. If I get volume 6 this week, I suspect I’ll be able to plow through the rest of the series more quickly.
