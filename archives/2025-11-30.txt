30 November Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

I’d been meaning to read “Project Hail Mary” for a while, then the movie trailer included what I was pretty sure are spoilers for the book. That’s my first piece of advice for those rare folks who want to read this but haven’t: avoid the movie trailer. Ryland Grace comes to on life support, unable to remember why he’s here, where he is, or even his own name. Slowly, step by step, he remembers and figures out what he’s supposed to be doing, aboard an interstellar ship far from home. This is my first Weir book. He writes an adventurous tale that builds up the mystery of what is going on, all while using the basic and not-so-basic tools of science to figure it out. And the story feels quite believable, except of course for the idea that the world would unify as it did to set this mission up.

-- Nonfiction --

-- Graphic Novels and Manga --

I picked up “The Rocketfellers” based upon a recommendation online. It starts with time travel, three generations of Rocketfellers running for their life. Landing in our current time in Olympia, Washington, the family assume new identities as they hide from the 25th century forces out to destroy them. And they clearly have secrets of their own. It’s a fun story, which doesn’t play heavy handed on the time difference. I will check out the second TPB when it releases next year instead of trying to catch up with individual issues.

Another online recommendation, “Wonder Woman: Outlaw” is the series refresh that started in 2023. The book certainly nods to a lot of existing Wonder Woman lore, but not so much that you feel lost for not having read it. After an Amazon kills every man in a bar, the United States starts violently rounding up the Amazonians in their territory. As the incel mentality takes hold of the country (well, more so), there is no room left for Wonder Woman. It’s a bit chilling reading this in 2025, though the original issues came out more than two years ago. I’ll definitely be picking up the next two TPB in the series soon.
