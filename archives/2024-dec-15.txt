15 December Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

“A Sorceress Comes to Call”, T. Kingfisher, is the third book now of hers I’ve read. She’s a great writer, and I will be seeking out more of her books in the coming year. This tale bounces between two points of view: Cordelia, whose mother is the titular sorceress, and Hester, the sister of the sorceress’ target. The heroes and villains here are all women, and Hester is well into her middle age. The story plays out nicely, though this isn’t the top Kingfisher book I’d recommend to someone getting started with the author.

-- Nonfiction --


-- Graphic Novels and Manga --

Five more volumes of “Chainsaw Man”, Vols. 11–15, Tatsuki Fujimoto. The first arc finishes up in volume 11, and the other volumes start a completely new story line. The complete lack of Denji at first was a bit disconcerting, as a second character is built up. The implications to this alternate universe of all the devils completely destroyed is interesting, but not dwelt on much at all, but popping out of the background here and there.
