20 April Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

“Never Let Me Go” is my third Kazuo Ishiguro book, and I dare say my favorite so far. It reads a bit slow, as we learn about the lives of children growing up in a boarding school somewhere in the English countryside. They study literature and are honored when their art is selected for “The Gallery”. But you can tell something is off, and the build up is so worth it, for a novel that questions so many of the topics you expect in literary fiction. It’s hard to say much more about the book, it’s best to go into it knowing nothing.

“The Spear Cuts Through Water” is a stand-alone fantasy novel with a distinct story structure, reading like a classic Greek play. There are three layers of story, with the central core story being the bulk of the book. I’ve come across quite a few recommendations for this, and those recommendations are well deserved. The two main characters are flawed but likable, and the world-building and twists play out in an engaging pace.

-- Nonfiction --

“No More Tears: The Dark Secrets of Johnson & Johnson” is an excellent examination of the largest healthcare conglomerate in the world. You will not come away from this unscathed. A moral society would not allow a company like this to exist. They knew the toxicity of talc since the 1950s; they knew the risks of acetaminophen, both in its dosage and packaging; they knew the dangers of the birth control patch; they knew the likely harm from metal-on-metal hip implants. The list goes on. Their opioids killed more people than the Sacklers, yet they’ve somehow avoided the same harm to their reputation. A must read, to bask in the glory that is for-profit healthcare.
