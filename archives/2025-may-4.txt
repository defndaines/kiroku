4 May Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

I forget how I heard about “I Cheerfully Refuse”. It’s a literary dystopian novel set in a near future on the shores of Lake Superior. Billionaires run society at their whim, and Rainy inadvertently crosses one. I got Willy Vlautin vibes, partially because the main character is a bass player who reminded me of the protagonist of “The Horse”. This story is more about the personal relationships, and the crumbling society feels more like setting than focus, though that isn’t a complaint.

“Nesting” takes second place for most stressful book I’ve read this year. This novel takes place in Dublin, 2018, as Ciara takes the bold step of leaving her emotionally abusive husband, with a four year old and two year old in tow. We experience the raw, internal struggle of the main character, as well as the hurdles she faces navigating support systems. Though the agency names are different, the situation will not come across as foreign in the least. It is not an easy read, but highly recommended. (And if you haven’t seen “Maid”, that’s a comp for this.)

-- Nonfiction --

-- Graphic Novels and Manga --

“よつばと!”, volume 16, continues the wonderful slice-of-life story of Yotsuba. They climb Mount Takao, and the visual renderings are gorgeous. The quirky humor is still going strong, though I could feel a bit of seriousness creeping in as her father is coming to terms with her starting school in the near future. I wonder if the artist will use that event to wrap up the story. I bought this in Japan, and this is the first book I’ve read in Japanese since I read the previous volumes back in November.

I really had no idea what “My Favorite Thing Is Monsters” was about going in. And, even when you settle into the story of a 10-year-old girl in 1968 Chicago, you still don’t realize where this story is going to go. There are some extremely emotional sections to this, especially with Karen’s family and her neighbor who survived the Holocaust. The art is beautiful, rendered as a spiral-bound notebook diary. Not lightweight material, but definitely recommended. I have a copy of the sequel.
