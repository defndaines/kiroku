27 April Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

I think I heard of “Hunchback” when it was long-listed for the International Booker Prize. Unfortunately, it didn’t make the cut for the shortlist. The author and main character have a similar disability, and this 112-page book explores issues of disability and sexuality in Japan. This book won the Bungakukai Prize for New Writers as well as the Akutagawa Prize. It’s a challenging book, but worth the read.

I may have read “The Joy Luck Club” before, or it could just be my memory of seeing the film over thirty years ago. As the story unfolded, a lot of it came back to me. I read this now because it’s required reading for my daughter’s English class. The four different mother-daughter pairs explore the tensions between first and second generation immigrants well, and I appreciated that some threads were left unfinished.

-- Nonfiction --

-- Graphic Novels and Manga --

“Gunnerkrigg Court, Volume 1: Orientation | Research” is an omnibus edition collecting the first 22 chapters of the webcomic (2005–2009). This is a reread for me, since I used to follow this regularly. I’m not sure why I stopped keeping up with it, though I have a bookmark that suggests I was reading through May 2019. The story and characters are fun, and I’ll probably try to catch back up once I have had a chance to clear some other graphic novels off my queue.

“The Summer Hikaru Died” is staying solid. I was a little worried I had forgotten too much of the tale, but it picked up nicely. It’s such an interesting idea, a mix of slice of life with a supernatural element. I’m looking forward to completing the series. The artist had originally planned six volumes, but I think it’s going to go one or two more. The sixth volume is already out in Japanese, but I’ll wait for the English on this one.
