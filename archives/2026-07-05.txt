## Fiction

This is my 4th book by Jones (not including comics), and this won too many awards not to read at some point. “The Buffalo Hunter Hunter” is framed across three time periods: a contemporary wrapping story; the journals of a Lutheran minister in 1912’s Miles City, Montana; and recollections relayed to that minister by Good Stab, a Blackfeet man who may have supernatural abilities. It’s an exploration of the scourge of white men pushing capitalism across the northern plains and what it means to survive that, wrapped in a horror story … er, well, more so. Definitely recommended.

“The Director” hit my radar when it was longlisted for the 2026 International Booker. I was hesitant, but positive reviews by others I trust got me to put in a hold. This is a fictionalized account of G.W. Pabst, an Austrian director, whose escape to Hollywood in the 1930s is cut short when he takes the fall for a bad movie and then is summoned back home to his mother. Desperate for talent, the Germans compel him to make movies, though he is sure he can submit only to his art. The book explores the kinds of people that rise to the top in regimes like this, alongside the personal compromises individuals make. At times the heavy-handedness comes across as farcical, but then I watch the news and realize it is probably honest. Not an uplifting story, but a story for our time.

## Manga

The nice thing about free comics through the library is that from time to time I can step outside my usual genres. “Bride of Ignat” is a BL tale set in a fantasy world, where every twenty years a “bride” must be sent up the mountain to appease the dragon, lest it destroy the village. Rita, already ostracized, hopes to find a place with the dragon, even though the dragon is quick to reject any need for companionship. The art is nice, and there are grains of an interesting story and world here. It is rather slow, though. I won’t be continuing, but I see the appeal.
