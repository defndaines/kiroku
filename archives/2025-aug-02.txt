2 August Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

I heard about “Misinterpretation” several weeks ago and was already reading it when the Booker longlist was announced. The author was born in Albania, and the story takes place between New York City and Albania. The narrator is an interpreter/translator who sometimes gets overly involved in the lives of those she is interpreting for, adding to the already strained relationship she has with her husband. This is not an easy book at times, and it doesn’t wrap things up in a bow at the end, but I’m glad I read it.

“Exit Strategy” is the fourth Murderbot book, following on from the last novella. SecUnit is trying to track down Dr. Mensah, but we know that’s not going to be simple. It’s hard to say much more without spoilers, but if you enjoy the Murderbot series, it is staying strong, and the character is developing is ways that keep it engaging. I should get a copy of the fifth book within a month.

-- Nonfiction --

-- Graphic Novels and Manga --

“Dr. Werthless” is a nonfiction graphic novel focused on the life of Dr. Fredric Wertham. This includes some background on famous cases that led to new ideas about psychiatry. Wertham was a complicated figure whose history now is deeply tied into his antagonism to comic books, which led to the comic code in the ’50s. But he was also associated with Richard Wright and other more progressive individuals. The art and lettering in the book are excellent, suiting the tone quite well. This is dense material, and readers should be warned that the descriptions of crimes borders on graphically nauseating at times.

“Dungeons & Dragons: Dungeon Club: Roll Call” continues my efforts to read Molly Knox Ostertag’s work. The protagonists in this one are beginning their 8th grade year, when the D.M. decides to start a club to expand the party. But Jess is fine as a party of one with her best friend crafting the stories. The book is a delightful mix of real-world issues and in-game play. Highly recommended to the target audience.

“Dungeons & Dragons: Dungeon Club: Time to Party” is part two, this time focusing on Olivia. Her sister in coming back from college on the weekends, but also given up her passion for playing music. Olivia has struggles of her own, some of them expressing through her dungeon mastering. And the two new party members have to integrate in, both in game and out. An excellent follow-up. I’m only sad to realize that the last book doesn’t come out until the end of September.
