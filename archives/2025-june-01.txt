1 June Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

“The Ministry of Time” is a Hugo finalist for best novel. The book was a nominee on a lot of lists for 2024 books, and won a couple awards (Goodreads Choice for Science Fiction). It takes place in the current time, with our main character recruited into the Ministry of Time in the U.K. to act as the bridge to one of six people pulled through time just before their deaths. What follows is a slow-build romance between her and her bridge, with a little bit of discussion of refugees (the main character and the author have Cambodian mothers), and a spy thriller dashed in. The time travel aspect is really just a backdrop, so I would barely qualify this as sci-fi. So far, I like this the least of all the novel finalists, a place I suspect it will hold, since I’ve heard good things about the last book I have still to read.
