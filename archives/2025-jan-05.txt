5 January Weekly Reading Wrap-up

-- Fiction --

“Funeral Songs for Dying Girls” starts a little slow, but then picks up. The main events of the novel take place the year Winifred turns sixteen. It’s a coming-of-age story mixed with coming to terms with death. It handles her relationships, mistakes, and reactions in a sensible manner that feels both realistic and healthy. This is the third book I’ve read by Dimaline, and I’m open to reading more as time allows.
